About

I am in my final year of my BSc (Hons) Computer Science and Artificial Intelligence at the University of Edinburgh. I have a passion for Space and using technology for good. Combining these passions, I am pursuing a career in the Aerospace Industry where I can have an impact globally.

A huge passion of mine is equality, diversity and inclusion (EDI) in STEM. I advocate for EDI in STEM in my positions as President of Edinburgh University Women in STEM and Director of Hello World Hack. I co-founded Hello World Hack in 2019 as I noticed a programming skills gap for students in most STEM degrees. After initial success, I have been Director (Co-Director in 2021) of Hello World Hack. Recently, I also co-authored a report on STEM studentsâ€™ experiences of EDI at the University of Edinburgh and, through this, have been working with the university to improve EDI awareness and education.

As a 2021 Brooke Owens Fellow, I will be joining Loft Orbital as a software engineering internb in Summer 2021

June 2019 - February 2021

Pixalytics Ltd. | Returning Software Engineer Intern

I first joined Pixalytics in 2019 as a summer Space Placement in Industry (SPIN) intern. SPIN is funded and managed by the UK Space Agency and it provides placmeent opportunities for students pursuiing a career in the space sector. The subsequent summer (2020), I returned to Pixalytics as a summer intern and then transitioned to work part-time during term.

At Pixalytics, I worked on a variety of projects. In my first summer, my focus was on maintaining and improving the water-extent and flood mapping product. Simultenously, I developed Flask APIs for several products, 3 of which have been deployed on the Pixalytics Portal. In 2020, my focus shifted to automating land cover classification with machine learning. I developed an approach using a SciKit-Learn support vector machine, and conducted research to compare this to the other developed methods.


April 2018

Amazon | Early Insights Intern

I participated in a week long event for a select group of girls studying Computing Science in the early years of their degree. over the week we attended talks and workshops and participated in a Hackathon to make a skill for Amazon Alexa. Me and my team created a game based on 'The Minister's Cat' (also called other names) using Python and Git for version control. This was created with the Alexa Tool Kit and Lambda (AWS).
